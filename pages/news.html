Common Grammar Mistakes to Avoid

April 15, 2025

Watch out for these common mistakes in english

Whether you're a beginner or an advanced English learner, grammar slips can sneak into your writing and speech. Here are some of the most common grammar mistakes I see students make—and how to fix them: One frequent issue is confusing "your" and "you're". "Your" shows possession (e.g., your book), while "you're" is short for "you are" (e.g., you're learning English). Another is subject-verb agreement, like saying "She go to school" instead of "She goes to school." Watch out for misplaced modifiers, which can make your sentence unclear, and remember that punctuation matters more than you think—especially commas!
